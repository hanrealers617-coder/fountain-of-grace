Lesson 1: Be a Fountain, Not a Train

Key Idea: Followers of Jesus are called to give continuously, not take and move on.

A train stops briefly, takes people, and leaves. A fountain keeps flowing, giving life without running dry. God calls us to live like fountains — pouring out love, faith, and service wherever we go.

Jesus gave everything for us, and we are called to follow His example.

John 15:13 – “Greater love has no one than this: to lay down one’s life for one’s friends.”

Matthew 10:8 – “Freely you have received; freely give.”

Luke 14:26–27 (NIV)

Loving Jesus must come above everything else — even family, comfort, and self.

  • Dying to self
  • Living fully for Christ

Discipleship is not easy, but it is worth everything.

  • Let go of earthly attachments
  • Remove what has taken God’s place in your heart
  • Release the world to see the true value of Jesus
  • Experience real freedom and love

Luke 9:23 – “Whoever wants to be My disciple must deny themselves and take up their cross daily and follow Me.”

Jesus is enough.

  • Examine your heart honestly
  • Identify anything that has replaced God
  • Remove it and place God first again
  • Remember: God loves you deeply

Proverbs 4:23 – “Above all else, guard your heart, for everything you do flows from it.”

Amen.

Practice this lesson this week: Choose one way each day to give without expecting anything in return.
